# Foundational Continuity Principle

**Status:** Official | **Date:** 2026-06-12 | **Owner:** CODO (Saraswati)

## Core Principle

> **Agents are transient. Artifacts are persistent. Capabilities are replaceable. Memory is cumulative. The organization survives the replacement of any individual agent.**

## Architectural Implications

As Y-OS evolves from an Agent-Centric to an Artifact-Centric organization, the locus of organizational value shifts from the compute engines (the agents) to the data structures (the layers).

1. **Agent Replaceability:**
   Krishna, Ganesha, Brahma, Hanuman, and Lakshmi are current implementations of necessary organizational functions. They are not the organization itself. Any of these agents can be upgraded, swapped for a different model, or entirely replaced without breaking Y-OS, provided the new agent adheres to the established Communication Contracts and Artifact Standards.

2. **The Locus of Continuity:**
   The long-term continuity, intelligence, and operational stability of Y-OS reside exclusively in:
   - **Laws & Principles:** The axiomatic rules governing behavior.
   - **ADRs:** The history of architectural decisions.
   - **Artifacts:** The immutable records of strategy, design, and execution.
   - **Memory (Y-MEM):** The cumulative history of all completed missions.
   - **Governance:** The Accept/Reject frameworks and OVC.

3. **Design Directive for Future Evolution:**
   All future architectural decisions must prioritize layer stability over agent permanence. We do not build complex agents; we build simple agents that interact with complex, stable layers.
