# 1. Artifact Layer v1

**Status:** Official | **Date:** 2026-06-12 | **Owner:** CODO (Saraswati)

## Core Hypothesis
Agents are transient and interchangeable compute engines. Artifacts are persistent states of knowledge and intent. Therefore, the true system of record in Y-OS is not the agent network, but the **Artifact Layer**. The organization communicates, remembers, and executes strictly through the state transitions of these artifacts.

## The Role of Artifacts in Y-OS
The Artifact Layer acts as the nervous system of Y-OS. It decouples the *actor* from the *action*. An agent does not directly command another agent; rather, an agent updates an artifact, and the state change of that artifact triggers the next agent in the Operational Value Chain.

## Connecting the Operational Value Chain
The Operational Value Chain is fundamentally an artifact lifecycle:

1. **Strategy Phase:** The CEO's intent is captured in the **Strategy Brief** (produced by Krishna).
2. **Execution Phase:** The Strategy Brief is consumed by Ganesha to produce the **Execution Plan**.
3. **Design Phase:** The Execution Plan is consumed by Brahma to produce the **Architecture Package**.
4. **Build Phase:** The Architecture Package is consumed by Hanuman to produce the **Build Artifact** and **Build Report**.
5. **Delivery Phase:** The Build Report is consumed by Ganesha to produce the **Delivery Report**.
6. **Learning Phase:** The entire chain of artifacts is consumed by Saraswati to produce the **Learning Report**.

Without the Artifact Layer, agents would rely on ephemeral conversational memory, leading to hallucination, scope creep, and execution failure. The Artifact Layer guarantees structural continuity from the CEO's initial thought to the final line of code.
