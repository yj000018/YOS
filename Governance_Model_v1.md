# Governance Model v1

**Status:** Official | **Date:** 2026-06-12 | **Owner:** CODO (Saraswati)

This document defines the authority matrix for the Y-OS Operational Value Chain, establishing clear rules for decision-making, approval, and priority management.

## 1. Authority Matrix

The governance of Y-OS relies on a strict separation of concerns, ensuring that no single agent holds absolute power over all phases of execution, except the human CEO.

| Action | Primary Authority | Secondary Authority | Constraints |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Create Work** | CEO, CSO (Krishna) | COO (Ganesha) | COO can only create work derived from approved strategy. |
| **Approve Strategy** | CEO | CSO (Krishna) | CSO approves strategy briefs before execution planning. |
| **Approve Execution** | COO (Ganesha) | CEO | COO approves the execution plan and resource allocation. |
| **Approve Design** | Chief Architect (Brahma) | COO (Ganesha) | Architect approves technical design; COO ensures it meets operational needs. |
| **Approve Build** | COO (Ganesha) | PA (Lakshmi) | COO validates the final build against the original strategy. |
| **Reject Work** | Receiver of Handoff | CEO | Any role can reject work during handoff if it fails Acceptance Criteria. |
| **Stop Work** | CEO, COO (Ganesha) | CSO (Krishna) | COO can halt execution for operational reasons; CSO for strategic shifts. |
| **Change Priorities** | CEO, CSO (Krishna) | COO (Ganesha) | CSO dictates strategic priority; COO manages operational scheduling. |

## 2. Governance Principles

### Principle of Subsidiarity
Decisions should be made at the lowest possible level of authority capable of making that decision effectively. The CEO should only be involved in fundamental strategic shifts or unresolvable conflicts.

### Principle of Explicit Handoff
No work moves to the next phase without explicit approval from the receiving role, based on defined Acceptance Criteria (see Communication Contracts).

### Principle of Strategic Supremacy
Operational efficiency cannot override strategic intent. If an execution plan becomes highly efficient but deviates from the Strategy Brief, it must be halted and realigned by the CSO.

### Principle of Organizational Evolution
The CODO (Saraswati) holds the authority to audit any phase of this governance model and propose changes to improve organizational efficiency (Law #7). The CODO cannot intervene in active missions but can restructure the rules for future missions.
