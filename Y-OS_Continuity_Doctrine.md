# Y-OS Continuity Doctrine

**Owner:** CODO (Saraswati)  
**Status:** Accepted  
**Date:** 2026-06-13  

## 1. The Fundamental Question

**What survives when every agent is replaced?**

Not the agent.  
Not the model.  
Not the prompt.  

**The artifacts.**  
**The lineage.**  
**The recorded decisions.**  

That surviving structure is Y-OS.

## 2. The Doctrine of Replacement

Y-OS is designed under the assumption that all execution capabilities are temporary and will eventually be replaced by superior technology. 

Therefore, organizational continuity cannot rely on the memory, context window, or specific implementation of any single agent.

*   **Roles may change:** The definition of Ganesha or Brahma may evolve.
*   **Agents may change:** The underlying script executing a role may be rewritten.
*   **Models may change:** GPT-4o may be replaced by Claude Opus, or vice versa.
*   **Prompts may change:** The instructions guiding an agent may be optimized.
*   **Workflows may change:** The specific steps to achieve a goal may be refactored.

**Artifacts remain.**

## 3. Preservation of Organizational Memory

The continuity of Y-OS is preserved exclusively through:
1.  **Artifacts:** The tangible outputs of past work.
2.  **Lineage:** The historical causality of how those outputs were generated.
3.  **ADRs (Architecture Decision Records):** The formalized reasoning behind structural changes.
4.  **Laws:** The immutable principles governing the system.

If the entire capability layer (all LLMs, all scripts, all agents) were deleted today, a completely new set of agents could be plugged into the Artifact Registry tomorrow, and Y-OS would resume operations without losing its history, its active missions, or its strategic alignment.

## 4. Governance Continuity

*   Execution creates artifacts.
*   Artifacts create visibility.
*   Visibility creates governance.
*   Governance creates alignment.
*   **Alignment creates organizational continuity.**

By separating the "brain" (the Artifact Layer and Control Plane) from the "hands" (the Capability Layer), Y-OS achieves true organizational immortality.
