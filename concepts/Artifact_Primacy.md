# Artifact Primacy

**Type:** Concept  
**Domain:** Constitution  
**Status:** CANONICAL  
**Constitutional Grounding:** Article I

---

## Definition

Artifact Primacy is the foundational principle of Y-OS stating that all organizational knowledge, decisions, and outputs must be materialized as explicit, versioned, and traceable artifacts. No knowledge exists unless it is an artifact. No decision is valid unless it is documented. No capability is real unless it is encoded. This principle prevents knowledge from residing only in agent memory or session context, ensuring that every meaningful act of organizational intelligence produces a durable, auditable record.
