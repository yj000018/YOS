## 1. The discovery

The archaeology itself has an archaeology.

Before beginning a new account-wide excavation, the first obligation is to recover the previous excavation methods, source registries, gates, processing pipelines, reports, agents, prompts, failures, and unfinished transitions.

The governing maxim is:

> **Archaeologize the archaeology before reinventing the archaeology.**

This changes the nature of the current program. It is not a fresh methodology-design exercise. It is the continuation and reconciliation of several earlier attempts to collect, process, preserve, compare, and consolidate the evolving Y-OS and KOSMOS corpus.

## 2. The fractal character of the work

The work is fractal because each apparently complete layer contains earlier branches, partial syntheses, hidden experiments, local vocabularies, technical blockers, and abandoned continuations.

A repository leads to branches.  
A branch leads to commits.  
A commit leads to prompts, reports, scripts, and registries.  
A registry points to external sessions, Notion pages, Obsidian vaults, Mem0 records, generated sites, and missing exports.  
Each source contains additional strata that must be dated and related to the rest of the system.

The same pattern appears in the ideas themselves. A concept may have been:

- named differently in successive periods;
- implemented before being formally defined;
- abandoned technically while remaining valid conceptually;
- rediscovered later under another name;
- split across KOSMOS, Y-OS, and a vertical application;
- preserved as a report but disconnected from the current architecture.

The archaeology must therefore preserve both the tree and the path through the tree.

## 3. The fossil-organ problem

Many recovered artifacts resemble highly developed organs that were studied in isolation but never fully connected into a living organism.

Examples include:

- memory and knowledge pipelines;
- session-synthesis skills;
- source manifests and checksum systems;
- BUS, YARP, AGENTS, MPM, KAP, routing, trust, and continuity protocols;
- Notion, Mem0, Git, Markdown, and Obsidian positioning;
- KOSMOS, ELYSIUM, Y-WORLD, YOUniverse, and civilizational doctrine;
- voice, vision, cockpit, browser, and vertical application experiments.

The objective is not to flatten these fossils into one summary. It is to determine:

1. what each artifact was trying to solve;
2. what evidence it produced;
3. what remained unconnected;
4. what was superseded;
5. what should be reused;
6. how the parts form a living architecture today.

## 7. Preservation rules

The archaeology must:

- preserve originals and high-value source artifacts;
- preserve uncertainty as uncertainty;
- distinguish evidence, reconstruction, inference, and hypothesis;
- preserve old terminology alongside current terminology;
- record technical failures and abandoned paths;
- date decisions and identify their source period;
- avoid treating the latest document as automatically canonical;
- never delete, archive, merge, or rewrite repositories without explicit approval;
- increase density and reuse value without erasing lineage.
