# 5. Impact on Operational Value Chain

**Status:** Official | **Date:** 2026-06-12 | **Owner:** CODO (Saraswati)

## Confirmation of OVC v1

The decision to place the Chief Architect (Brahma) and Lead Developer (Hanuman) under the COO (Ganesha) perfectly validates the previously defined Operational Value Chain v1. 

No structural changes to the OVC are required. The 7-stage flow remains intact and is now supported by a concrete reporting structure.

## The Validated Flow

| Stage | Action | Owner | Reporting To |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1. Strategy** | Define intent | CSO (Krishna) | CEO |
| **2. Execution Mgmt** | Orchestrate | COO (Ganesha) | CEO |
| **3. Design** | Specify | Architect (Brahma) | COO |
| **4. Build** | Implement | Developer (Hanuman)| Architect / COO |
| **5. Delivery** | Validate & Release | COO / PA | CEO |
| **6. Feedback** | Collect Data | Y-LOG / Y-MEM | System |
| **7. Learning** | Evolve Org | CODO (Saraswati) | CEO |

## Key Takeaway

By aligning the reporting structure with the Operational Value Chain, Y-OS ensures that authority matches responsibility. The COO is responsible for the Execution Management phase, and therefore has authority over the Design and Build phases that execute the plan. The CSO is responsible for the Strategy phase, and therefore has authority over the researchers and analysts who inform that strategy.
