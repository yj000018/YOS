# ADR-0009: Formalization of the Operational Value Chain

**Status:** Accepted
**Date:** 2026-06-12
**Author:** CODO (Saraswati)
**Context:** Y-OS possesses an executive structure (CEO, COO, CODO, CSO) and an organizational mandate (Laws #7, #8, #9), but lacks a formal, deterministic process for converting strategic intent into operational execution. Without this, execution risks becoming siloed, unaligned with strategy, or immune to systematic improvement.

## Decision

We adopt the **Y-OS Operational Value Chain v1** as the canonical flow for all missions.

This value chain mandates a strict 7-stage progression:
1. Strategy (CSO)
2. Execution Management (COO)
3. Design (Architect)
4. Build (Operator)
5. Delivery (COO/PA)
6. Feedback (Y-LOG/Y-MEM)
7. Learning (CODO)

Furthermore, we enact **Y-OS Law #10**:

> **Law #10: The Operational Value Chain is immutable. No execution may bypass Strategy, and no delivery may bypass Learning. The flow of intent to execution to evolution must remain unbroken.**

## Rationale

- **Alignment:** Ensures that all execution is directly tied to a formally approved Strategy Brief.
- **Accountability:** Clarifies boundaries between roles (e.g., the Strategist does not execute; the Architect does not build).
- **Evolution:** Structurally embeds the Learning phase (Phase 7) into every mission, guaranteeing compliance with Law #7 (continuous improvement).
- **Scalability:** Provides a universal template that applies to software, publishing, business, and personal domains, allowing new roles to be slotted into the existing chain without redesigning the core process.

## Consequences

- All future missions must explicitly identify their current stage in the Value Chain.
- Handoffs between roles must adhere to the defined Communication Contracts (Acceptance/Rejection criteria).
- The CODO is empowered to audit any mission to ensure compliance with the Value Chain and Law #10.
