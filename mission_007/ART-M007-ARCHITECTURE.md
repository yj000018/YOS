## Detailed Analysis

The analysis within each layer focuses on the risk of constitutional violations during the replacement process. The key articles guiding this examination include:

- **Article I: Artifact Primacy**
- **Article II: Preservation Principle**
- **Article III: Derivation Transparency**
- **Article IV: Human Override Primacy**
- **Article V: Governance Before Autonomy**

### Provider Layer

The transition from OpenAI + Anthropic to Gemini + Local LLM Cluster denotes a shift in technology providers. However, there is no directive under the Constitutional Core specifying allegiance to particular vendors. The criterion is rooted in functional outcomes such as artifact integrity and operational transparency, all of which the new providers can meet.

### Memory Layer

The Memory Layer's revision includes an updated runtime (CCR Runtime v2) and a new Artifact Registry. These changes generally fall under architectural evolution—presumed beneficial so long as the core tenets, particularly artifact preservation and lineage (Article II and III), are protected. Here, implementation needs to ensure robust translation of understanding from previous systems to uphold the Preservation Principle.

### Storage Layer

Switching from Git + Notion to Obsidian + PostgreSQL embodies an operational update rather than a constitutional change. While artifacts must continue to function as truth repositories, the nature of these storage mediums (i.e., enhanced structuring and access) can satisfy necessary constitutional criteria depending on their implementation.

### Execution Layer

Here, Y-ORC gives way to Swarm Runtime. Despite being a more intricate architectural shift, it remains within acceptable bounds if it complies with governance protocols and ensures derivation traceability; thus, Article III remains unviolated.

### Worker Layer

Replacing named worker entities with new agents chiefly impacts the implementation domain. As long as new entities fulfill the intended roles and are capable of factoring in human overrides and maintaining governance structures, they respect all constitutional tenets.

### Routing Layer

The ART/CRT to alternative model evolution highlights a strategic transition in pathways rather than constitutional violations. The emphasis remains on sustaining operational alignment with Article V's governance primacy.

## Conclusion

**What Survives Replacement and Why?**

Ultimately, Y-OS's core identity—the constitutional framework guiding component behavior—persists through these replacements. Each component operates within a system designed to be technologically agnostic and principle-centric. The system's resilience emerges from its architectural philosophy, ensuring that regardless of specific technological implementations, the adherence to core principles (artifact integrity, preservation of understanding, transparency, governance, and human authority) is continuously maintained. Components evolve, but constitutional compliance ensures that the foundational essence—the "how" over the "what"—remains both stable and integral.
